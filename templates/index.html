DATASCI W209 Data Visualization

Project Concept

Our group would like to visualize FEMA declared disasters from the mid twentieth century alongside climate change data that describes changes in the Earth’s temperature. The reason why we were drawn to this idea was that despite the prevalence of climate change visualizations, there were not many visualizations that linked climate change to government action. For our project concept, we want to not only show changes in the environment but also how those changes may have forced the US government to react through the proxy of FEMA declared disasters.

Additionally, our project would allow users to explore FEMA disasters by state and type, over a set period of time. This would educate the audience on the frequency changes of environmental disasters.

Users

Our users would consist of environmental policymakers and advocacy groups who are interested in understanding the relationship between climate change and FEMA declared disasters.

Visualization Tasks

Users will be able to explore FEMA disasters as a nation or by state. Depending on the level of granularity, users will be able to see the number and type of disasters within a given year or time period. Juxtaposed with this FEMA data, we’ll also indicate the changes in global climate temperatures and state any correlation.

Data Sources

  • US Natural Disaster FEMA Declaration on Kaggle
  • EPA Greenhouse Gas Data Explorer
  • EPA Greenhouse Gas Inventory
